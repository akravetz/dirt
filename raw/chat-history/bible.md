### Environmental Targets by Phase

| Phase | Temp (Day) | Temp (Night) | RH | VPD |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Seedling** | 75–80°F | 70°F | 65–75% | 0.4–0.8 kPa |
| **Veg** | 75–82°F | 68–72°F | 55–65% | 0.8–1.2 kPa |
| **Early Flower** | 72–80°F | 65–70°F | 45–55% | 1.0–1.5 kPa |
| **Late Flower** | 68–75°F | 62–68°F | 35–45% | 1.5–2.0 kPa |

> **Denver Note:** Ambient room temps (67°F night / 72°F day) are good but slightly cool for seedlings. The humidifier is essential early on — Denver's dry air can pull tent RH down to 20–30% without supplementation. Denver tap water uses chloramines (not free chlorine) — these do not off-gas. Always pH Down to 5.5–6.0 before adding to reservoir.

## 5. Grow Timeline (Phase-by-Phase)

> Note: Total timeline will be refined once strain flowering time is confirmed.

### Phase 1 — Germination & Seedling (Week 1–2)
- Germinate in Rapid Rooter cubes under seedling dome
- 24/0 light schedule during seedling phase, then transition to 18/6
- Keep humidity high (65–75%) — use humidifier
- No nutrients needed at this stage — coco is inert but seedlings don't need feeding yet
- Transition to Medic Grow Fold-650 at 10–15% once dome LEDs prove insufficient
- **Milestone:** 2–3 sets of true leaves visible; roots visible at base of cube

### Phase 2 — Transplant to Autopots / Early Veg (Week 2–4)
- Transplant Rapid Rooter cube directly into coco/perlite-filled Autopot
- Hand water from the top with Canna Coco A+B at low EC (~0.8–1.0) for first 2–3 weeks
- **Do not activate Autopot float valve yet** — roots need to reach the bottom third of the pot
- 18/6 light schedule, ramp to 40–50% intensity
- **Milestone:** Plant has 4–5 nodes, roots visible at pot base

### Phase 3 — Topping & LST (Week 4–6)
- **Single top at node 4–5** — clean cut above the node with sterile scissors
- Allow 5–7 days recovery
- Begin LST: tie down main branches horizontally to open canopy
- Activate Autopots once plants are established and drinking consistently (float valve on)
- Increase Canna Coco A+B EC gradually as plant size increases
- **Milestone:** Two main colas developing, plant responding to LST

### Phase 4 — Late Veg / SCROG Fill (Week 6–9)
- Install SCROG net 18" above pots
- Weave branches through net as they grow — goal is flat, even canopy
- Continue 18/6 light at 60–70% intensity
- **Flip to 12/12 when net is ~70% filled**
- **Milestone:** SCROG net 70% covered

### Phase 5 — Flower Stretch (Flower Week 1–3)
- Switch to 12/12
- Expect 50–100% height increase (strain-dependent) — monitor closely
- Continue tucking new growth under net for first 2–3 weeks of flower
- Maintain Canna Coco A+B at mid-range EC — no product change needed
- Begin dialing down humidity toward 45–50%
- **Milestone:** Stretch complete, pistils visible everywhere

### Phase 6 — Mid Flower (Flower Week 4–7)
- Maintain Canna Coco A+B, EC appropriate to plant size and response
- Maintain RH 45–50%, temps 72–78°F
- Defoliate lightly at week 4 to improve light penetration
- Check reservoir weekly — this is peak water consumption
- **Milestone:** Buds stacking, trichomes developing

### Phase 7 — Late Flower / Ripening (Flower Week 7–Harvest)
- Flush reservoir and run plain pH-adjusted water for final 1–2 weeks
- Drop RH to 35–45% — critical for mold prevention and trichome production
- Drop temps slightly at night to encourage purple coloration and trichome production
- Monitor trichomes with jeweler's loupe or digital microscope
- **Harvest when:** 70–90% cloudy trichomes, 10–20% amber (personal preference)
- **Clone keeper plant before harvest** for Grow #2

### Phase 8 — Harvest, Dry & Cure
- Harvest entire plant or branch-by-branch
- Hang dry in dark, 60°F / 60% RH environment for 10–14 days
- Jar cure: 62% Boveda packs, burp jars 2x/day for first 2 weeks
- Minimum cure: 4 weeks | Optimal cure: 8+ weeks for boutique quality
- **Milestone:** Stems snap cleanly, buds smell complex and fresh

## 6. Autopot System — Operating Guide

### Initial Setup
1. Fill Autopot pots with 60/40 coco/perlite mix — do not compress
2. Transplant Rapid Rooter cube into center of pot, backfill around it
3. Hand water from the top with pH-adjusted nutrient solution (pH 5.5–6.0)
4. Keep float valve **closed** for first 2–3 weeks post-transplant
5. Open float valve once roots have reached the bottom third of the pot and plant is drinking consistently
6. From this point: fill reservoir, dial in EC and pH, walk away

### Reservoir Management
- **pH target:** 5.5–6.0 (coco range) — check and adjust every reservoir fill
- **Denver tap note:** Tap pH runs 8.5–8.8 due to the city's Lead Reduction Program. Use General Hydroponics pH Down every fill. Chloramines in Denver water do not off-gas — no need to let water sit.
- **EC targets (approximate):**

| Stage | Target EC |
|---|---|
| Early Veg | 0.8–1.0 |
| Late Veg | 1.2–1.6 |
| Flower | 1.6–2.0 |
| Late Flower / Flush | 0.0 (plain water) |

### Reservoir Top-Off Schedule (Estimated)

| Stage | Daily Consumption | 47L Lasts | 100L Lasts |
|---|---|---|---|
| Early Veg | ~0.5L/plant | ~20 days | ~45 days |
| Late Veg | ~1L/plant | ~10 days | ~25 days |
| Flower Peak | ~2L/plant | ~5 days | ~12 days |

> **Week-Away Tip:** In peak flower, a 100L reservoir with 4 plants gives ~10–12 days before a top-off is needed. Plan any travel around this window. 47L will not be sufficient during peak flower for week-away periods — upgrade recommended.

### What Goes In The Reservoir
- pH-adjusted water (5.5–6.0) + Canna Coco A+B at target EC
- Mix A and B separately before combining — never add A and B directly to each other
- During final 1–2 weeks of flower: plain pH-adjusted water only (flush)

## 8. Training Reference Guide

### Approach: Single Top → LST → SCROG

This grow uses a simplified training plan optimized for low maintenance and pheno expression visibility.

#### Step 1: Single Top
- Wait until plant has **4–5 nodes**
- Cut cleanly above the node with sterile scissors
- Result: 2 main shoots develop
- Allow 5–7 days recovery before further stress

#### Step 2: LST (Low Stress Training)
- Gently tie down main branches horizontally using soft plant ties or pipe cleaners
- Goal: open canopy, expose bud sites, keep height manageable
- Adjust ties every few days as plant grows
- No further topping — keep it simple

#### Step 3: SCROG
- Install net when plant reaches ~12" tall
- As branches grow, weave horizontally through net squares
- Goal: flat, even canopy across the entire 4x4
- Flip light to 12/12 when **70% of net is covered**
- Continue tucking for first 2–3 weeks of flower (stretch phase)
- Stop tucking once buds start forming
