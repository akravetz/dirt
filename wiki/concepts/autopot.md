# Autopot System

## Setup
- **System:** Autopot 4-Pot XL, 15L pots
- **Reservoir:** 25-gallon FlexiTank Pro (21"×21" base, ~208 lbs full)
- **Reservoir stand:** Oak step stool, 6" height — see [Reservoir Stand decision](../decisions/2026-04-11-reservoir-stand.md)
- **Mechanism:** Gravity-fed; float valve in each tray controls water flow from reservoir — no electricity, no pump

## Hand-Watering Phase (Current)

**Float valve must remain CLOSED for the first 2–3 weeks post-transplant.** Roots must colonize the bottom third of the pot before Autopot is activated.

**Current watering protocol:**
- 150–200ml per plant
- Targeted at stem base (tight 4" radius)
- Every 1–2 days
- Remove any runoff from trays immediately (turkey baster, sponge, or tipping) — standing water risks anaerobic conditions before roots reach that zone

## AirBase Disc

The Autopot XL pot ships with a **gold AirBase fabric disc** at the base of the pot. This disc aerates the root zone — but it also means **visual root confirmation is not possible**. You cannot check whether roots have reached the pot bottom by looking at the drainage holes. Do not attempt to remove or lift the disc to check.

**Visual root checks do not work with this setup. Activation readiness must be determined entirely from behavioral signals.**

## Float Valve Activation — When and How

### When to Activate

Hand-water for the first **2–3 weeks after transplant**, or until roots have colonized the bottom of the pot (~20 days from transplant). We transplanted around **Mar 29**, so the activation window is approximately **Apr 12–19**.

**Signs roots are ready — behavioral indicators only:**

| Signal | What to look for | Why it indicates root readiness |
|--------|-----------------|--------------------------------|
| **Watering frequency** | Pot drying out faster than a week ago — needing water daily instead of every other day | Root mass has expanded; more surface area drawing moisture through the medium |
| **Growth rate acceleration** | New nodes appearing every 2–3 days instead of 4–5 | Roots keeping up with above-ground demand; plants entering exponential veg growth |
| **Pot weight test** | Lift pot right after watering, then again 24 hours later — if dramatically lighter, plant is drinking well and roots are distributed through the medium | Water weight loss = root-driven uptake, not just surface evaporation |

Use all three signals together, not just one. A plant that drinks fast AND is pushing nodes rapidly AND loses pot weight quickly is ready. A plant that only shows one signal may not be.

### Transition Procedure

**Activate one plant first as a test before opening all four valves.**

The safest strategy is to activate the healthiest, most stable plant first, confirm the system is cycling correctly, then activate the remaining three.

#### Step 1 — Test plant activation
1. Choose the most vigorous, problem-free plant (currently Plant B or D — avoid Plant C until its leaf issue is resolved)
2. Do a **final thorough hand-water** on the test plant — 400–500ml at pH 5.8 / current EC, ensuring even saturation
3. **Fill the reservoir** with pH-adjusted nutrient solution at the same EC you've been hand-watering with — do not change concentration at transition
4. **Open the test plant's float valve only**
5. Monitor the tray over the next 24–48 hours — watch for the AquaValve cycling:
   - ✅ **Tray fills → plant drinks → tray empties → valve refills** = roots are ready; system is working
   - ❌ **Tray fills and stays full for hours; coco surface is wet and waterlogged** = roots haven't reached the tray zone yet; close the valve and wait 3–5 more days before retrying

#### Step 2 — Activate remaining plants
6. Once the test plant shows confirmed cycling over 24–48 hours, perform final hand-waters on the remaining plants and open their valves
7. Check all four trays 2–3 times per day for the first 48 hours to confirm each is cycling independently

### Common First-Activation Mistakes

| Mistake | Consequence | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| Activating too early (roots not established) | Plant can't drink fast enough; coco stays waterlogged | Wait for behavioral readiness signals |
| Not cleaning AquaValve before first use | Debris prevents proper seating; tray overflows | Rinse valve before installation |
| Reservoir pH drift | pH creep stresses roots | Check and adjust daily for first week; weekly thereafter |
| Changing EC at transition | Sudden concentration shift stresses plant | Match reservoir EC to what you were hand-watering with |

### Coco-Specific Note

The AquaValve works by letting all tray water be consumed before refilling. In coco, the medium wicks efficiently, so the cycle is **faster than in soil** — don't be alarmed if the tray empties and refills frequently. Monitor the top inch of coco for **salt buildup** (white crust on surface) — even with autopots, occasional top-water flush may be needed to prevent salt accumulation at the surface. See [Coco Coir](coco-coir.md).

## Float Valve Activation (Ongoing Operation)

Once activated:
1. Fill reservoir with pH-adjusted Canna A+B solution
2. Open float valve — system self-regulates
3. Check reservoir level weekly (more frequently at peak flower)
4. Refill and re-adjust pH/EC at every top-off

## Reservoir Management (Post-Activation)

| Stage | Daily Consumption | 100L Lasts |
|-------|------------------|------------|
| Early Veg | ~0.5L/plant | ~45 days |
| Late Veg | ~1L/plant | ~25 days |
| Flower Peak | ~2L/plant | ~12 days |

**Week-away capability:** 100L reservoir gives ~10–12 days at peak flower — plan travel around this window.

## Ongoing Drainage / Cleaning Cadence

Autopot is continuous-feed with no runoff, which creates three distinct maintenance needs that hand-watering doesn't have. Missing any of them leads to silent problems (root rot, pH drift, salt accumulation).

### 1. Tray drydown — weekly

The AquaValve keeps ~1–2 cm of nutrient solution in the tray at all times. Continuous wet = anaerobic root zone = root rot risk. Roots need oxygen as much as water.

**Procedure — close all float valves for 24–48 hours once per week:**
- Plant drinks the tray dry, then pulls moisture from the coco via transpiration.
- Tray bottom goes visibly dry; coco surface goes pale-brown.
- Reopen valves; normal cycle resumes.

Autopot's own guidance recommends this weekly. Coco wicks fast, so 48 hours gives a genuine dry-back at the root zone during peak veg/flower.

### 2. Reservoir change — weekly to 10-day

Topping off is not sufficient. Over days, the solution degrades:

- **pH creep** — typically drifts upward; by day 7 expect 0.2–0.4 pH shift from fresh.
- **Salt precipitation** — dissolved nutrients fall out of solution, especially calcium phosphate if pH drifts high.
- **Biological growth** — algae (if any light reaches the tank), bacteria/fungi in warm water regardless.
- **Selective uptake drift** — plants pull N:P:K in different ratios than the mix provides; what's left over is no longer balanced.

**Full change every 7 days (baseline) or up to 10 days** if the reservoir is opaque, cool, and measured pH/EC are still in range. Process: drain remaining solution (siphon or dump), quick rinse with clean tap water, refill with fresh Canna mix.

Canna's own recommendation is weekly. Do not reuse leftovers as starter.

### 3. Top-water flush — every 2–4 weeks

Unlike hand-watering, autopot delivers from below with **zero runoff**, so there is no flush cycle pushing accumulated salts out of the coco. Salts build up in the medium and can form a visible white crust on the coco surface.

**Every 2–4 weeks, hand-water from the top** with plain pH 5.8 water (or very weak Canna at EC 0.4) — ~500ml per pot. Measure runoff EC from the tray: if significantly higher than the reservoir, buildup was real. Close valves afterward to let the (now full) tray dry back — this doubles as the weekly drydown.

### Suggested weekly rhythm

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| Mon | Reservoir change (drain + refill fresh) |
| Tue–Sat | Plants drinking; top off reservoir as level drops |
| Sun | Close valves → tray drydown (24–48h) |
| Mon | Reopen valves + next reservoir change |

Every 2–4 weeks, insert a top-water flush on any day. Log each drydown and reservoir change in the day's daily entry so drift over time is traceable.

## pH/EC Targets
- pH: 5.5–6.0 (target 5.8) — GH pH Down required every fill (Denver tap pH 8.5+)
- EC: 0.8–1.0 early veg → 1.6–2.0 flower. See [EC concept](ec.md) for full autopot reservoir chart.
