# SCROG — Screen of Green

SCROG uses a horizontal net/screen installed above the plants to create a flat, even canopy. Branches are woven through the net squares as they grow, maximizing light exposure across the entire 4x4 footprint.

## How It Works

1. Install net when plants reach ~12" tall (estimated weeks 6–8 of veg for this grow)
2. As branches grow through net squares, tuck and weave them horizontally
3. Goal: flat, even canopy across the entire 4x4
4. Flip to 12/12 when **~70% of the net is covered**
5. Continue tucking for first 2–3 weeks of flower (stretch phase)
6. Stop tucking once buds begin forming

## In This Grow

- **Net:** VIVOSUN 4x4 Trellis Net
- **Installed:** 2026-04-18 (Day 35, Day 6–7 post-topping) — 11" above the canopy / **18" above pot base** (matches plan spec). Installed ahead of the original weeks-6–8 estimate; the net now serves as an anchor plane for upcoming LST ties rather than a later constraint.
- **Flip trigger:** 70% net coverage

## Key Notes
- SCROG works best after topping — the two main shoots spread naturally into the net
- Flip timing is critical: too early = wasted veg time; too late = overgrown net pre-flip
- During stretch (first 2–3 weeks of flower), continue tucking new growth under net
