# Topping

Topping is a high-stress training (HST) technique where the apical meristem (growing tip) is cut to break apical dominance and split one main stem into two. It is the first step in this grow's training sequence: **Single Top → LST → SCROG**.

## How It Works

1. Allow the plant to grow to node 4–5 (the "topping trigger")
2. Using sterilized scissors or a razor, cut the main stem cleanly above node 4
3. The plant redirects growth hormones (auxins) to the two axillary shoots at the top node
4. These two shoots become co-dominant main stems, effectively doubling the canopy

## Why Top

- **Breaks apical dominance** — without topping, one main cola dominates and shades the rest
- **Creates a wider, more even canopy** — two main stems spread naturally before LST and SCROG
- **Increases yield** — more top-level bud sites instead of one central cola
- **Keeps height manageable** — critical in a 4x4 tent with fixed light height

## When to Top (This Grow)

- **Trigger:** Node 5 emerging (node 4 established with internodes visible)
- **Plant A status:** Topping trigger met as of Day 28 (Apr 11) — **top Apr 13–15**
- **Recovery:** 5–7 days. Resume LST after recovery on the two resulting main shoots.
- **Only one top** — this grow uses a single top per plant, not multiple topping rounds

## Procedure

1. Sterilize scissors/razor with isopropyl alcohol
2. Cut main stem cleanly above node 4 (leave a short stub)
3. Do NOT top during lights-off or when the plant is stressed
4. Monitor for 5–7 days — drooping for 1–2 days is normal
5. Begin LST once the two new shoots are 2–3 inches long

## Risks

- Stresses the plant — avoid topping a plant that is already stressed (e.g., Plant C with active pH issues)
- Slows vertical growth for ~1 week during recovery
- If cut too low (below node 3), recovery is slower and the plant may bush excessively

## In the Training Sequence

```
Single Top (node 4–5) → 5–7 day recovery → LST (spread two main shoots) → SCROG net install
```
