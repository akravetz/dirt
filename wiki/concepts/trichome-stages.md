# Trichome Stages & Harvest Timing

Trichomes are the resin-producing glands on cannabis flowers. Reading their development stage is the most reliable method for determining optimal harvest timing — more reliable than the breeder's stated week count, which varies by environment.

---

## Trichome Types

Cannabis produces three types of trichomes. Only one matters for harvest timing:

| Type | Size | Location | Significance |
|------|------|----------|--------------|
| **Bulbous** | Microscopic (a few μm) | Entire plant surface | Secretes resin; poorly understood; not useful for harvest reading |
| **Capitate-sessile** | Small; flat against surface | Leaf undersides, stems | Produces small amounts of cannabinoids; develops pre-flower |
| **Capitate-stalked** | 50–100 μm; mushroom-shaped | Primarily on calyxes and sugar leaves | **Primary THC/CBD/terpene factory; the ones you read at harvest** |

For harvest timing, focus exclusively on **capitate-stalked trichomes on the calyxes** (bud tissue), not on fan leaves or sugar leaves — those trichomes mature earlier and will give a falsely late reading.

---

## The Three Ripening Stages

### Stage 1 — Clear (Immature)
- Trichome heads are transparent, glass-like
- THC is still accumulating; harvesting now produces weak, anxious, heady effect
- **Action:** Wait — weeks of potency left to develop

### Stage 2 — Cloudy / Milky (Peak THC)
- Heads turn opaque white-milky
- THC content is at or near maximum; terpene profile is fully developed
- Effect: cerebral, energetic, uplifting
- Cloudy trichomes persist for approximately **1–2 weeks** before amber conversion begins
- **Action:** This is the harvest window for peak potency

### Stage 3 — Amber (THC Degrading to CBN)
- Heads shift from white to gold/amber
- THC is converting to CBN (cannabinol) — a degradation product with sedative, narcotic properties
- Effect becomes progressively more body-heavy, couch-lock, sleepy
- **Action:** Harvest when desired amber ratio is reached (see targets below)

---

## Harvest Timing Targets

| Goal | Trichome Ratio | Effect Profile |
|------|---------------|----------------|
| Maximum potency / concentrate | 85–90% cloudy, 5–10% amber | Peak cerebral, energetic |
| Balanced / most common | 70–80% cloudy, 20–30% amber | Well-rounded; potent with some body |
| Relaxation / sedation | 50–60% cloudy, 40–50% amber | Heavy body, couch-lock |

For a **pheno hunt focused on terpene complexity and bag appeal** (this grow's goal), the 70–80% cloudy / 20–30% amber range is typical — enough CBN contribution to round the effect without losing terpene peak.

---

## How to Check

**Equipment:** 30–60x jeweler's loupe or a digital microscope (60–100x). The Photone app does not work for trichomes. A phone camera with macro mode can work in a pinch but lacks the resolution for confident readings.

**Technique:**
1. Check **calyxes on the apical cola** (top bud) — this matures last and sets the harvest trigger
2. Also check **a lower bud** — these mature earlier; useful as an early warning system
3. Take 3–5 readings across different bud sites per check session
4. Check every 1–2 days once the window approaches; trichomes can move through cloudy→amber in under a week in warm conditions

**Denver note:** Cool ambient nights in late flower will slow trichome maturation slightly, extending the harvest window — an advantage for dialing in the exact ratio.

## Relevance to This Grow's Pheno Hunt

At flower weeks 5–6, the final phenotype evaluation uses trichome development as one of the primary signals. The evaluation criteria for selecting a keeper (from the [anthocyanin decision](../decisions/2026-04-01-anthocyanin-priority.md)):

1. **Purple calyx depth** — anthocyanin expression in actual bud tissue
2. **Aroma complexity** — evaluated at peak terpene (cloudy trichomes)
3. **Bud structure and density**
4. **Stretch behavior**
5. **Overall plant health**

Trichome stage affects criteria 1, 2, and 3 directly — purple is most visible when trichomes are cloudy (not amber, which adds an orange/brown cast), and terpenes are most aromatic at peak cloudy.

**Clone timing implication:** Clones must be taken no later than flower weeks 3–4, well before trichome ripening begins. The trichome check at weeks 5–6 is for final evaluation of the original plant — not the clone.

---

## Amber Expression vs. Anthocyanin Expression

These are distinct and can coexist, but they interact visually:
- **Anthocyanin** (purple) is in the plant tissue itself — calyxes, leaves, stems
- **Amber trichomes** sit on top of that tissue and add a gold/orange overlay
- At high amber percentages, the amber trichome coat can partially mask purple calyx color in photos
- For pheno evaluation, check purple calyx color in areas where you've blown trichomes away, or at an angle
