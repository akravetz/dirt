# Anthocyanin (Purple Expression)

Anthocyanins are water-soluble pigments that produce red, purple, and blue colors in plant tissue. In cannabis, they appear in leaves, stems, petioles, and — most desirably — buds.

## Environmental vs. Genetic Expression

| Type | Trigger | Significance |
|------|---------|--------------|
| Environmental | Cool temps (below ~65°F), UV stress | Any plant can express purple if conditions are right; does NOT predict purple buds |
| **Genetic** | No trigger required; present at ambient/warm temps | **Strong indicator for purple bud phenotype** |

**Key principle:** Anthocyanin appearing BEFORE environmental triggers (cool temps, UV stress) = pure genetic expression → reliable predictor of purple bud development.

## In This Grow

Plants A and D began showing anthocyanin at ambient temperatures during early veg. This was identified as genetic expression — appearing in stems, petioles, leaf undersides, and even cotyledons.

- **Plant A:** Purple stem/leaf undersides first noted Day 16 (March 30). Upgraded to confirmed genetic expression by Day 22 (new growth tips showing color at ambient temps). See [2026-03-30](../daily/2026-03-30.md), [2026-04-01](../daily/2026-04-01.md).
- **Plant D:** No purple noted at Day 16. Strongest signal confirmed Day 18 (April 1) — stem, petioles, cotyledon pigmentation all showing color before any environmental trigger. See [2026-04-01](../daily/2026-04-01.md).

## Denver Advantage

Denver's cool ambient nights (~67°F) will **amplify anthocyanin expression in late flower** for any plant with genetic predisposition. This is a natural advantage for this grow's purple pheno hunt goal.

## Final Evaluation Criteria

Early anthocyanin is a promising signal but NOT the sole selection criterion. Final keeper determination at flower weeks 5–6 based on:
1. Purple depth in calyxes specifically (not just leaves)
2. Aroma complexity
3. Bud structure and density
4. Manageable stretch behavior
5. Overall plant health
