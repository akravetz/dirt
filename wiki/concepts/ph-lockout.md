# pH Lockout

pH lockout (also called nutrient lockout) occurs when the root zone pH drifts outside the optimal range, preventing the plant from absorbing certain nutrients even though those nutrients are present in the solution. In coco coir, the optimal pH range is **5.5–6.0** (target 5.8).

## How It Happens

1. Nutrient solution is mixed at the correct pH (5.8)
2. Over time, salt buildup in the coco, inconsistent pH adjustment, or Denver's high-pH tap water (8.5–8.8) causes the root zone pH to drift
3. At pH below 5.5 or above 6.5, specific nutrients become chemically unavailable
4. The plant shows deficiency symptoms even though the nutrient solution contains adequate levels

## Symptoms

- **Brown/rust spots** on older/lower leaves (calcium or magnesium lockout)
- **Yellowing between veins** (iron or manganese lockout at high pH)
- **Leaf edge burn or curling** (potassium or phosphorus lockout)
- **Stunted new growth** (multiple nutrients locked out simultaneously)
- Symptoms appear on older leaves first (mobile nutrients) or new growth (immobile nutrients) depending on which element is locked out

## pH Ranges and Nutrient Availability (Coco)

| pH | Effect |
|----|--------|
| Below 5.0 | Toxic — aluminum/manganese toxicity risk |
| 5.0–5.5 | Low end — iron, manganese available; calcium starts to lock |
| **5.5–6.0** | **Optimal range for coco** — all nutrients available |
| 6.0–6.5 | Calcium/magnesium still available; iron starts to lock |
| Above 6.5 | Multiple lockouts — iron, manganese, zinc, copper |

## Diagnosis

1. **Measure runoff pH and EC** — water the plant with 300–400ml at pH 5.8, collect tray runoff
2. **Compare input vs runoff pH** — if runoff pH is significantly higher or lower than input, the root zone has drifted
3. **Compare input vs runoff EC** — high runoff EC (much higher than input) indicates salt buildup

## Correction

1. **If runoff pH outside 5.5–6.2:** Flush with 2–3x pot volume of plain pH 5.8 water
2. **If runoff EC is very high:** Flush to reduce salt buildup, then resume feeding at lower EC
3. **After flushing:** Resume normal feeding at correct pH and reduced EC (0.8–1.0 for early veg)
4. **Monitor daily** for 3–5 days — symptoms may take time to resolve even after correction
