# Lollipopping & Defoliation

Two distinct but complementary techniques applied at the transition to flower and through early flowering. Both redirect plant energy from low-value growth to productive bud sites at the canopy level.

---

## Lollipopping

### What It Is
Removing all growth from the **bottom third to half** of the plant — bud sites, small branches, and fan leaves — leaving the upper canopy untouched. The result looks like a lollipop: full and leafy on top, bare stem on the bottom.

### Why It Works
Light intensity drops sharply with distance from the source (inverse square law). Growth below the canopy plane receives 10–20% of the light at the top. These shaded lower bud sites consume plant energy and produce "popcorn" buds — small, loose, and low-value — while diverting energy from the productive upper canopy.

Removing them:
- Forces energy into the top bud sites → bigger, denser upper colas
- Dramatically improves airflow under the canopy → reduces mold risk
- Reduces overall leaf mass → more energy available for flower

### When to Lollipop
- **Photoperiod plants:** At or just before 12/12 flip — typically **0–3 days before flipping**
- Can also be done in the final week of veg if plants are already large
- Do NOT lollipop after week 3 of flower — the plant is no longer in a recovery-capable state; stress at this point reduces yield

### How to Lollipop
1. Identify the canopy plane (the screen level in a SCROG grow)
2. Remove all branches and bud sites **below** the screen — these will never reach productive light
3. On branches that do reach through the screen, strip the lower nodes of that branch (the parts still under the net)
4. Make clean cuts at 45° close to the stem; 45° angle allows water to roll off the cut surface
5. Work from the bottom up; remove one plant at a time and allow a day or two before doing the next if plants show stress

### SCROG Integration
In a SCROG grow, lollipopping is especially important because the screen creates a hard canopy boundary. Everything below the net is in deep shade by definition. At flip:
1. Lift the net if possible, lollipop the lower third, replace the net
2. Or lollipop through the net squares by reaching underneath
3. After lollipopping, the first 2–3 weeks of stretch can be managed by tucking new growth through remaining open squares

## Defoliation

### What It Is
Selective removal of **fan leaves** (large shade leaves) throughout the canopy to open light channels to lower bud sites and improve airflow.

Distinct from lollipopping: defoliation targets specific fan leaves anywhere on the plant, not wholesale removal of lower growth.

### Why It Works
Dense fan leaves in a filled SCROG canopy block light from penetrating to the bud sites below them. Removing them:
- Opens vertical light channels through the canopy
- Improves airflow → reduces humidity pockets and botrytis risk in flower
- Slightly stresses the plant → research suggests mild stress may increase trichome density (see [Plant Training Methods](plant-training-methods.md))

### When to Defoliate

| Timing | Approach | Notes |
|--------|---------|-------|
| Late veg / day of flip | Moderate defoliation | Plant is in recovery mode; heals fast |
| Flower week 1–2 | Light to moderate | Remove fan leaves blocking bud sites |
| Flower week 3 | Light only — tuck instead of pull | Plant's energy is shifting to flower production |
| After week 3.5 | Stop removing leaves | Defoliation after this point reduces yield |

**The week 3 hard stop** is widely cited: beyond week 3–3.5 of flower, the plant cannot meaningfully recover energy from defoliation stress, and removing leaves reduces photosynthetic capacity during active bud development.

### How to Defoliate
1. Identify fan leaves that are **shading active bud sites** — not all fan leaves, only blocking ones
2. Pull with a firm, quick snap (cleaner than scissors for large petioles); or cut close to the stem
3. Do not remove more than 20–30% of the leaf mass in a single session
4. Spread heavy defoliation over 2–3 days if removing many leaves
5. Never defoliate a stressed, sick, or recovering plant

---

## Combined Approach for This Grow

At 12/12 flip:
1. **Lollipop first** — remove everything below the SCROG net
2. **Defoliate second** — selectively remove fan leaves blocking bud sites above the net
3. Allow 3–5 days recovery before any further leaf removal
4. **Flower weeks 1–3:** Light defoliation as needed; tuck aggressively
5. **After week 3:** Stop. Tuck only.

**Timing reminder:** Flip is planned when SCROG net is ~70% full (estimated late April / early May). Lollipopping and defoliation happen at that flip.
