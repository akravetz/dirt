# Decision: Anthocyanin Priority Shift

**Date:** 2026-04-01
**Status:** Active

## What Was Decided

Plants A and D elevated to **primary keeper candidates** based on confirmed genetic anthocyanin expression.

Plants B and C deprioritized to secondary status — still healthy and vigorous, but no purple signal.

## Evidence

Both A and D showing deep purple coloration on leaf undersides, stems, and petioles **at ambient temperatures** (~70–76°F) with no environmental triggers applied. This confirms genetic origin rather than stress response.

D shows the strongest signal including cotyledon pigmentation. A's signal confirmed upgraded to "genetic" by April 5 when new growth tips showed color.

## Reasoning

Anthocyanin expression before environmental triggers is the most reliable early-stage predictor for purple bud phenotype. The goal of this grow is a dark purple phenotype with exceptional bag appeal — A and D represent the highest-probability path to that outcome.

## Constraints

- All 4 plants continue to grow — no culling at this stage
- Final selection deferred to flower weeks 5–6 (purple calyx depth, aroma, bud structure, stretch, health)
- Clone commitment no later than flower weeks 3–4
- A and D are primary but B and C remain in the running — early signal ≠ guaranteed winner
