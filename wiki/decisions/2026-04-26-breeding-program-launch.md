# Breeding Program Launch — Two-Track SBxBS01 F2 Selection

Today we launch a deliberate breeding program built on the Oregon Breeding Group seed packs (Sirius Black × BS01). This decision page records the program's structure, rationale, and scope so the protocol pages in [`breeding/`](../breeding/README.md) have a stable foundation.

## What we're doing

Run a small two-track home breeding program over the next ~9 months:

- **Track A — pollen production:** germinate ~5 SBxBS01 regular seeds (already on hand from the original OBG order). Sex at ~3–4 weeks. Flip surviving males immediately. Evaluate, pick the best male, collect pollen, freeze in aliquots for multi-year storage.
- **Track B — phenotype hunt:** after the current grow harvests, germinate a 10-pack (second pack, BS line TBD) and pheno-hunt for keeper female(s). Clone every plant before flip.
- **F2 cross:** clone of the keeper female × banked pollen from Track A → F2 seeds. Targets ~150–500 seeds from a 2-branch pollination event.

The current grow's 4 plants (A/B/C/D) are themselves SBxBS01 F1s and become *de facto* additional pheno candidates — clones are taken this week so they remain viable as cross candidates.

## Why now

- Wayne (OBG breeder) included the regulars specifically for buyers to do exactly this. We didn't realize until 2026-04-24 that the regulars were a deliberate breeding-stock allocation. See [`concepts/oregon-breeding-group.md`](../concepts/oregon-breeding-group.md).
- Pollen banks for ≥18–24 months frozen — we can collect pollen now and use it any time in the next 1–2 years.
- The current grow is in mid-veg; we have a natural quiet window to set up cloning + male isolation before the main tent flips.

## Why this structure (not alternatives)

Considered and rejected:

| Alternative | Why not |
|---|---|
| **Buy a feminized-only second pack and skip males entirely** | Regulars came free with the order; throwing them away is wasted genetics. Also: no path to making our own seed without males. |
| **Selfing (S1) via colloidal silver** | Adds chemistry, hermie risk, and only produces same-line offspring. Misses the point of having BS01 regulars on hand. |
| **Whole-plant pollination of a keeper** | Wastes the smokable harvest. Selective branch pollination (1–2 branches) gives both seed and bud. |
| **Pop the 10-pack and the regulars in parallel right now** | Tent is already full with the current grow; not enough space. Sequence them: regulars now (small footprint, isolation room), 10-pack after current harvest. |
| **Skip pheno-hunting and just cross any female with any male** | Defeats the purpose of having a 10-pack for selection. F2 hunt loses its goal if parent selection is random. |
| **Aim for full strain stabilization (IBL) on first cycle** | 4–6+ generations, multi-year project. Premature commitment given we haven't yet identified a phenotype worth stabilizing. F2 with selection is the right first move; stabilization is a future decision. |

## Trait priorities

In priority order (informs male and female selection):

1. **Color (anthocyanin expression)** — primary axis. BS01 was selected for it, plants A and D currently demonstrate it. See [`concepts/anthocyanin.md`](../concepts/anthocyanin.md).
2. **Terpene profile** — primary; subjective but high-impact on enjoyment.
3. **Structure (tight internodes, bushy)** — primary; matters for our 2x2 / 4x4 space constraints.
4. **Finish time** — secondary tiebreaker.
5. **Vigor** — secondary tiebreaker.
6. **Resilience** — long-term, deferred to multi-cycle observation.

Hermaphrodite tendency = automatic disqualifier on either side.

## Open questions

These are NOT blocked on the program launch but need resolution before specific phases:

- **Second pack (Track B) — which BS line?** Options on the breeder's site: SB×BS01 (same line as current — refinement path), SB×BS13 (purple, similar to BS01), SB×BS35 (pink/purple/green, aromatic), SB×BS45 (striking colors + diesel terps — most distinct). Preliminary lean: BS45 for trait distance, but user is considering. Resolution target: before mid-July.
- **Keeper female source** — could come from current grow's clones (A/B/C/D) or from the 10-pack hunt. Decision deferred until both groups have been scored.
- **Number of cross directions** — single direction (M_SBxBS01 × F_SBxBSxx) or both (also M_SBxBSxx × F_SBxBS01)? Both is cheap if regulars from the second pack are available; provides comparison data.

## Scope explicitly excluded

The following are deliberately *not* part of this program:

- Genomic sequencing as a selection criterion (deferred per [`concepts/oregon-breeding-group.md`](../concepts/oregon-breeding-group.md) — phenotype matrix is the primary selection signal at this scale).
- Commercial seed production or distribution.
- Stabilization beyond F3 (revisit after F2 hunt completes).
- Backcrossing to original parents (P generation) — we don't have them.
- New cross outside the OBG-supplied lines.

## Hardware acquisitions required

- Cloning station (humidity dome, rooter cubes, hormone, light, heat mat) — ~$100–130 one-time.
- Dedicated portable freezer for pollen storage — set up in isolation room.
- Surgical masks, paintbrushes, parchment paper, gloves, plant tags — minor consumables.

## Critical-path commitments

These are gating items. Slippage on any cascades through the rest of the program:

1. **Cloning station up + clones taken from current 4 plants this week** — without rooted clones before the imminent flip, A/B/C/D are unrecoverable as breeding candidates after harvest.
2. **Regulars germinated this week** — pollen bank needs ~7–8 weeks from germ to frozen aliquots; the keeper female's cross window opens late November.
3. **Isolation room established before regulars sex** — males cannot be in the main tent at any time, and they'll show sex around 2026-05-22 if popped now.
