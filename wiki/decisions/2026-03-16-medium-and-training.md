# Decision: Medium, Nutrients & Training Approach

**Date:** 2026-03-16
**Status:** Final

## Medium

**Chosen:** Coco/perlite 60/40

**Rejected alternatives:**
- Living Organic Soil — harder to manage in Autopot system; less control over nutrients; messier deficiency correction
- DWC — too complex, too much maintenance; not week-away friendly

**Rationale:** Best balance of control (vs. soil), simplicity (vs. DWC), and Autopot compatibility.

## Nutrients

**Chosen:** Canna Coco A+B (sole product, covers veg and flower)

**Rejected alternatives:**
- Gaia Green dry amendments — incompatible with Autopot gravity-fed system
- Multi-product nutrient stacks — over-engineered; Canna A+B sufficient

**Key constraint:** Denver tap water pH 8.5–8.8 requires GH pH Down at every fill. Canna A+B's buffering does NOT eliminate this need.

## Training Method

**Chosen:** Single top at node 4–5 → LST → SCROG

**Rejected alternatives:**
- Mainlining — over-engineered for this grow
- Multiple topping — unnecessary complexity
- No training — would waste 4x4 light footprint

**Rationale:** Simplified to match low-maintenance, week-away-capable goal. Single top creates two main colas; LST manages structure; SCROG fills the 4x4 for maximum yield.

## Cloning Strategy

Run all 4 plants through flower weeks 5–6. Evaluate: purple calyx depth, aroma, bud structure, stretch, health. Clone top candidate(s) no later than flower weeks 3–4 for Grow #2.

Early culling based on a single indicator (e.g., only purple signal) is risky — wait for multiple signals before making final selection.
