# Cloning

Cloning is the **safety net for the entire breeding program.** Every irreversible decision (flipping a plant to flower, harvesting, killing a male) becomes much less risky if a rooted clone exists. Without clones, a phenotype is gone the moment you flip her.

This page is the practical procedure for our setup. The principles generalize, but the equipment and space numbers are specific to a small home grow.

## Why we clone everything (in the breeding context)

- **Pheno hunt insurance** — a "keeper" is identified at harvest, weeks after the flip-to-flower decision is irrevocable. Without clones, the keeper is gone before you knew she existed.
- **Cross is on a clone, not the mother** — when we pollinate, we pollinate a *clone* of the keeper. The original mother stays unpollinated and produces normal smokable flower. This decouples "I want to smoke this plant's bud" from "I want to make seeds with this plant."
- **Multi-generation continuity** — if we like the F2 in two years, we can re-cross *exactly the same parent* by reviving its clone, instead of having to re-find an equivalent phenotype from F1 seed.

## Space requirements

Footprint of an active dome: 10×20 inches = ~1.5 sq ft + clearance. A single dome holds 36–50 cuttings comfortably.

**Mother stations (rooted clones in veg)** — bigger ongoing footprint:

| Pots | Typical pot size | Approx footprint | Light |
|---|---|---|---|
| 4 mothers (current grow) | 1 gal | 4 sq ft | 100W LED |
| 10 mothers (hunt) | 1 gal | ~10 sq ft | 200W LED |

Realistic minimum space for the program: a 2×4 ft shelf or small closet that can hold one active dome and ~4–6 1-gallon pots under a 100–150W LED at 18/6.

### Stasis mode vs. productive-mother mode

There are two operating modes for clones, and they need different space + medium decisions:

- **Stasis mode** — the program just needs the genetics alive while a longer process plays out (e.g., waiting for pheno hunt results). Clones stay in **16oz solo cups**, get minimal water and no nutrients, and grow imperceptibly. A closet shelf with a 30–50W LED bar at 18/6 is enough for 8–16 clones in stasis. **For this grow, the 8 clones taken from A/B/C/D will live in stasis on a closet shelf in another room from 2026-05 through ~2026-08 until the keeper is identified.**
- **Productive-mother mode** — once a keeper is named, *that one* gets up-potted to 1-gal, moved under stronger light, fed normally, and run as the donor for the next grow's cuttings. The other clones in stasis can be culled at that point.

### Medium choice for stasis pots

For stasis-mode solo cups, **buffered peat-based soil (e.g., FoxFarm Happy Frog) is preferred over coco/perlite**. Reason: stasis mode is about *infrequent attention*. Coco demands daily watering; soil holds moisture for 3–4 days, lets you ignore the shelf safely while attention is elsewhere (current grow, male tent, pheno-hunt prep). The $15 cost of one small soil bag is worth the lower-effort path.

For productive-mother mode (post-keeper-selection), match whatever the next grow runs in (likely coco/perlite to match the main tent).

**Mothers can be aggressively pruned** to keep them small — top them, lollipop them, hold them at ~12" tall by selectively removing growth tips. They produce more cuttings per pruning event when kept compact. (Stasis-mode clones don't even need this — they're not producing, just surviving.)

### Where the cloning station lives in this setup

The cloning dome and stasis-mode solo cups go on **a closet shelf in another room — not inside any tent.** The main tent stays focused on the current grow, and the male 2×2×4 isolation tent (per [isolation.md](isolation.md)) is dedicated to males + flowering keeper clone, not cuttings. Cuttings + young plants near a flowering male is a contamination risk and an environmental mismatch (high humidity dome + low humidity male tent don't mix in one space).

## Procedure (full)

### Day 0 — take the cutting

1. **Choose donor** — a healthy, vigorous mid-lower branch on the mother. Avoid the very top (apical, harder to root) and the very bottom (often weaker stems). 4–6" long with 2–3 nodes is ideal.
2. **Pre-soak rooter cubes** in pH 5.8 RO water for 30 min. Squeeze out excess but don't crush.
3. **Sterilize scissors/razor** with isopropyl alcohol. Wipe dry.
4. **Make the cut** at a 45° angle just below a node on the donor branch. Keep the cut surface oriented up so it doesn't dry on the way to the rooter.
5. **Strip the lower leaves** — anything that would touch the rooter or be inside the dome cup. Optionally trim remaining leaves in half (reduces transpiration during the rootless phase). Don't strip everything; the cutting needs photosynthesis.
6. **Dip the cut end** in Clonex gel — coat the bottom 1/2" thoroughly. The pink dye makes coverage obvious.
7. **Insert into the rooter cube** by pre-poking a hole with a pencil (don't shove the gel-coated stem in directly — strips the gel off). Insert deep enough that the cutting stands upright on its own.
8. **Place rooter+cutting in the tray**, mist the leaves lightly, close the dome with vents *fully closed*.

### Days 1–3 — high humidity phase

- Keep dome closed, vents shut.
- Mist leaves once daily (morning).
- Light at low intensity, 18/6. Place light high enough that it's not heating the dome.
- Heat mat should hold the rooter cubes at 75–78 °F. Check with a probe thermometer.
- Don't peek more than once a day.

### Days 4–7 — vent acclimation

- Crack vents 25%. Mist every other day.
- Look for visible white root tips poking out of the rooter cube. Earliest sightings ~day 5–6 on aggressive strains; 10–14 days is typical.

### Days 7–14 — rooting

- Vents 50–75% open as cuttings show vigor.
- Stop misting once roots are visible.
- Cuttings that haven't shown roots by day 14 are usually failures. Discard if they yellow or wilt.

### Day 14–21 — transplant

- When roots are 2–3" long and visibly healthy: transplant to **16oz solo cups of Happy Frog soil for stasis mode** (default for this program — see "Stasis mode vs. productive-mother mode" above), OR to a 1-gal pot in coco/perlite if going straight into productive-mother mode.
- Harden off out of the dome over 2–3 days: leave outside the dome for 2 hours, then 4, then 8, then full removal.
- Stasis: move to closet shelf under 30–50W LED at 18/6, water sparingly, no nutrients for the first 2 weeks then very light feed (~EC 0.6) every other watering only. Productive: move under stronger light (100–150 PPFD) and start watering with light nutrients (~EC 0.6–0.8).

## Take rate expectations

- **70–90%** on healthy stock with good technique.
- **50–70%** on stressed mothers, dull blades, or improperly soaked rooters.
- **<50%** = something's wrong. Likely culprits: rooter too dry, scissors not sterile, cuttings from very-bottom suckers, or mother in flower (cuttings from flowering plants take longer and revert phenotype).

**Take 2× the cuttings you actually need.** For 4 keeper-candidate plants (current grow), take 8–12 cuttings total. For 10 hunt plants, take 20–30.

## Mother management — keeping a clone alive long-term

A rooted clone in veg under 18/6 light can live for years. To keep her productive and small:

- **Top regularly** to keep height under control. Each topping cut becomes a new clone donor branch.
- **Lollipop** lower growth that's not productive cutting material.
- **Re-pot every 6–12 months** to refresh root zone, or run hydro/coco with regular flushing.
- **Light requirements**: 100–200 PPFD at canopy is enough for a non-flowering mother. Don't waste flower-grade light.
- **Nutrients**: light feed (EC 0.8–1.0) keeps her healthy without lush growth. We're not optimizing for yield.

## Keeping clone identity straight

This is where the program lives or dies — a misidentified clone makes the cross unrepeatable.

- **Label every cutting at insertion** with the mother ID (e.g., `A1`, `A2`, `D1`, `D2`).
- Use plant tags or paint-pen on the rooter cell wall.
- After transplant, label the pot itself with the mother ID + clone date.
- In the wiki/journal, note which clones rooted from which cutting events.
- **If a clone's label falls off and you can't recover it: discard.** A clone with unknown parentage is worse than no clone — it can contaminate the cross record.
