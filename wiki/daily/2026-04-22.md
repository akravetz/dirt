## Summary

Firmware ready; hardware deployment pending solder-up.

## Issues & Flags

### ✅ Overnight environment in veg target — both metrics simultaneously (first time)
Overnight RH 52.06% is within the 45–55% veg target (was 74.37% on Apr 20) and overnight temp 70.17°F is within the 68–72°F veg night range (was 66.84°F). Both overnight metrics in target simultaneously for the first time this grow. Confirms the `dirt-hwd` service restart activated the lights-off feedforward: humidifier is forced OFF for the pre-lights-off prep window and the VPD setpoint is reduced during lights-off.

### ⚠️ Afternoon temperature below floor (72.34°F; target 74–76°F)
The 14:00 temp is 1.66°F below the veg day floor. Morning avg (73.94°F) is also just under 74°F. Tent is running slightly cool compared to Apr 20 (75.04°F). The ~10 hPa pressure drop since Apr 20 (841 → 831 hPa) suggests a weather system is passing through Denver, cooling the ambient. Monitor over the next 1–2 days; if temp stays below floor, reduce exhaust fan speed slightly during lights-on.

### ⚠️ Afternoon RH elevated (69.19%; target 55–65%)
The 14:00 RH is 4 points above the 65% ceiling. VPD (0.84 kPa) is within the 0.8–1.2 target due to the lower temperature — so the plants are not in immediate stress. The elevated RH + lower temp combination is worth watching: if the afternoon temp does not recover to 74–76°F, the humidifier may be slightly over-running. The dew point at 61.66°F is high enough that any further temperature drop could push overnight condensation risk up.

### ✅ Plant D color improving
Today's photo shows Plant D in a lighter medium-green — a meaningful improvement over the "pale yellow-green" noted Apr 20. Leaf structure is clean (no spots, curl, or edge damage). The transient color may have been related to moisture or nutrient flux as the autopot cycle established. Continue monitoring through the reservoir change.

### ⚠️ Plant A overnight sensor dropout
Plant A has no overnight moisture data (null, n=0 for the 00–06 window). Morning and afternoon readings are present and stable (~53%). The ESP32-C3 node likely rebooted or lost WiFi during the overnight window. No action needed if the node is stable now; verify it is posting normally.

### ⚠️ Plant C moisture very high (75.75%, trending up from 72% overnight)
Coco at 75%+ continuously is at the high end of the tolerable range. In an autopot system, near-constant wicking is expected, but sustained high moisture without a dry-down cycle can increase anaerobic root zone risk over time. No symptoms visible today. Monitor for yellowing, wilting, sliminess at root collar, or a sour smell from the medium.

### 🔴 Reservoir change due (Day 7 post-activation)
The Autopot FlexiTank was activated Apr 15. Today is Day 7 — the start of the 7–10 day recommended first change window. Change at the next opportunity: drain remaining solution, rinse reservoir, refill with fresh Canna A+B at EC 0.8–1.0; pH to 5.8 after nutrients; Denver tap pH 8.5–8.8 requires GH pH Down at every fill.

### 🟡 LST status (Day 11/10 post-topping)
Plants A (Day 11) and B/C/D (Day 10) are past the ideal LST window. Photos do not show clear anchor ties on branches. If LST has not been started, begin immediately — shoots become progressively harder to bend without snap risk and the SCROG canopy zone is being underutilized.
