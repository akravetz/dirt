### VPD Swing Incident

The humidifier was off at some point today, causing RH to drop to **42%** and VPD to spike to **~2.03 kPa** (at 80°F/42% RH). Once the humidifier was restored, RH returned to 70% and VPD settled at ~0.89 kPa.

A swing of 2.03 → 0.89 kPa in a short period is significant stress — stomata cycle rapidly between open and closed, disrupting transpiration and nutrient uptake. This kind of oscillation is harder on plants than a steady suboptimal value. **Keep the humidifier running consistently.** Target 65–70% RH at current temps.

## Plant C — Day 25 ⚠️ ESCALATED

**Symptom progression confirmed — requires prompt action.**

Brown/rust-colored spots now visible on **multiple leaves**, particularly older/lower fan leaves. This is a worsening from the lighter-green + edge-spotting flagged on Day 22. The spotting is more defined and spreading to additional leaf surfaces.

**New observation:** Purple coloration now visible on stems and petioles. This is a **first-time observation for Plant C** and the timing/context matters:
- A and D showed purple at ambient temps with no health issues → genetic expression
- C's purple appears concurrent with worsening stress symptoms → **likely stress-induced anthocyanin**, NOT genetic expression
- Stress triggers (pH lockout, nutrient deficiency, root zone issues) can cause any plant to express anthocyanin — this does NOT elevate C's keeper priority

**Differential diagnosis for brown/rust spotting + lighter green:**

| Cause | Pattern | Likelihood |
|-------|---------|------------|
| **pH lockout** (most likely) | Interveinal spots, older leaves first; stunted uptake of multiple nutrients | High — Denver tap + hand-watering drift |
| **Calcium deficiency** | Brown spots, curling edges, affects newer growth too | Medium — coco depletes Ca without adequate supply |
| **Manganese deficiency** | Interveinal chlorosis (yellow between veins, brown spots) | Medium — locked out below pH 5.5 |
| **Overwatering** | General yellowing, drooping, anaerobic odor at surface | Low — 150–200ml per watering |

**Most probable diagnosis: pH lockout** — if watering pH has drifted above 6.2 or below 5.4, multiple nutrients lock out simultaneously producing exactly this symptom cluster. Denver tap at 8.5–8.8 requires GH pH Down at every fill; any missed adjustment would cause this.

**Required action — check Plant C runoff pH and EC today or tomorrow:**
1. Water C with 300–400ml at pH 5.8 / EC 0.8–1.0
2. Collect runoff from tray (do not discard before measuring)
3. Measure runoff pH and EC
4. Target runoff: pH 5.5–6.2, EC not more than 0.3 above input
5. If runoff pH is outside 5.5–6.2: flush C with 500ml plain pH 5.8 water, then resume normal feeding

**Status:** ⚠️ Escalated. Active diagnosis in progress. Plant is functioning but leaf damage is real. Run C in parallel with A/B/D through flower — do not cull, but do not elevate priority based on stress-induced purple.
