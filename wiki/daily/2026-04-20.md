## Issues & Flags

### ✅ Daytime temperature in target (75.04°F / 74.43°F morning avg)
Both the 14:00 reading and morning avg are within the 74–76°F veg day target — a meaningful improvement over yesterday's 78.58°F / 76.09°F.

### ✅ Daytime VPD in target at 14:00 (1.12 kPa)
VPD at 14:00 is 1.12 kPa — within the 0.8–1.2 veg target for the first time. The morning avg (1.41 kPa) is still above the ceiling, but the afternoon trend confirms the humidifier is effectively managing VPD during the lights-on period.

### ⚠️ Overnight RH regressed (74.37% vs 70.79% yesterday)
Overnight RH ticked back up from 70.79% to 74.37% and overnight VPD dropped from 0.68 → 0.57 kPa. Overnight temp also slipped to 66.84°F (from 68.0°F). This regression is directly attributable to the `dirt-hwd` service not yet having been restarted — the lights-off feedforward deployed Apr 19 (forces humidifier off for 30 min pre-lights-off, subtracts 0.3 kPa from the VPD band at night) cannot activate until the service restarts. **Action: `systemctl --user restart dirt-hwd` before tonight's lights-off.**

### ⚠️ Plant D lighter green than peers
Plant D appears noticeably lighter/paler green in today's photos vs. B and C. Leaf structure is healthy (no spots, curl, or edge burn). Could be mild N availability at this autopot pod, a phenotypic trait, or early light response. Monitor over next 2–3 observations; if it progresses toward yellow or shows curling, check autopot float valve flow.

### 🔴 LST still outstanding (Day 9/8 post-topping)
All four plants remain untrained — Day 9 for A, Day 8 for B/C/D. Shoots are well-sized and should be bent before they lignify. Every additional day without LST makes the bend harder and reduces how much of the SCROG net can be used effectively.

### ⚠️ BME280 stuck-state incident at 17:36 MDT
Sanity-check against a handheld hygrometer revealed the tent-hub BME280 was reporting 43.5% RH while the handheld showed 63% — a ~20 point gap. Temp was close (74.6°F sensor / 73°F handheld). The stale-sensor failsafe did not trip because readings were updating; they were just wrong. `systemctl --user restart dirt-hwd` (which toggles DTR → resets the Arduino → re-inits the BME280 over I²C) cleared the stuck state. First post-restart sample at 17:37: 61.2% RH / 75.0°F / VPD 1.15 kPa — agrees with the handheld within ±2 points. The pre-restart VPD of 1.65 kPa was driving the humidifier harder than warranted. This is the **second** recurrence of this pattern; full incident log lives in [hardware/humidifier-control.md](../hardware/humidifier-control.md#bme280-stuck-state-recurring).
