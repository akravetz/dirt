## Flags

### Lights-on VPD improved, but overnight remains too wet

Compared with May 3, humidity dropped from roughly 70-74% to 61-64% and morning VPD moved into target at 1.12 kPa. That is real progress after the flip. The overnight window is still below target at 0.76 kPa, and the current reading is slightly warm/dry at 80.53°F / 1.33 kPa, so the next control goal is a smoother day/night profile rather than more humidity.

### Plant B is still the main plant-level risk

B is now 96.13%, up from 95.51% yesterday and 94.93% on May 2. The photo still does not show obvious wilt, but the trend says dryback action has not taken hold. Keep the B float valve closed or otherwise interrupted, and manually confirm tray water, valve behavior, and sensor plausibility.

### C and D remain saturated

C is still about 90% and D about 87%. Both have active upper growth, but lower/interior yellowing is visible under a crowded SCROG canopy. Inspect tray standing water and airflow before changing nutrients.

## Recommendations

1. **Keep drying the tent overnight** - morning/now VPD is usable, but the 00-06 window is still wet for early flower. Review lights-off humidifier gating and exhaust/air exchange so overnight VPD approaches 1.0 kPa without pushing the day period above 1.3 kPa.
2. **Escalate Plant B dryback verification** - B is 96.13% now and rose again. Confirm the float valve/tray is actually off or restricted, check for standing water, and only reopen after a measurable dryback or a proven sensor fault.
3. **Tuck A and D daily during stretch** - keep tall sativa-leaning shoots under the net while the canopy is still pliable.
4. **Thin only dead or blocking lower leaves for airflow** - do not do a hard defoliation yet; remove only leaves that are dead, trapped against wet media, or blocking essential airflow.
5. **Verify early-flower reservoir targets** - next reservoir change should confirm pH near 5.8 and EC appropriate for early flower before assuming the lower-leaf symptoms are feed-strength related.
