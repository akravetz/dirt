## Issues & Flags

### 🟡 Overnight temperature drop (63.54°F avg)
The lights-off window averages 63.54°F — ~10°F below the daytime reading and below the veg night target of 68–72°F. The grow light is the tent's primary heat source; when it cuts off, tent temp tracks toward ambient room temperature (~67°F Denver night). Plants tolerate 60°F transiently without visible damage, but sustained cool nights slow veg growth rate. Note for later: Denver's naturally cool nights will actively help amplify anthocyanin expression in late flower — a net advantage for this pheno hunt. For now, warmer nights would accelerate veg. Options: reduce exhaust fan speed at lights-off, or put a small space heater on a timer.

### 🟡 Overnight RH and VPD swing (76.95% / 0.46 kPa overnight)
With temp dropping and the humidifier still running, RH spikes to ~77% overnight. At 63°F the saturation point is lower, so the same absolute moisture content produces higher relative humidity. The closed-loop humidifier service is targeting 60% RH without a time schedule — lowering the nighttime setpoint to ~55% (or pausing during lights-off) would bring overnight VPD into range without under-humidifying during the light period.

The morning-to-now VPD of 1.17–1.31 kPa is acceptable but slightly above the 0.8–1.2 target ceiling; this is partly a consequence of the overnight humidifier overcorrecting and then the morning lights-on window drying things back out.

### 🟡 Plant B morning moisture drydown (3.57% avg, 07:00–14:00)
Plant B went from 32.13% overnight → 3.57% for the full 7-hour morning window (n=838) → 23.89% by 14:00. This is consistent with a scheduled tray drydown (the recommended weekly dry cycle per the autopot protocol) or a natural float-valve cycle where the tray went empty and refilled. The recovery to 23.89% by 14:00 suggests the tray refilled. Not alarming given autopot has been active since Apr 15; monitor B's moisture over the next 24h to confirm normal cycle.

### 🟢 Plant A/D sensor data gaps (expected)
Null overnight/morning data for A and D is expected — v1.2 sensors replaced today; old (unreliable) historical data purged. The "now" readings (A: 33.79%, D: 22.44%) are from freshly calibrated v2.0 sensors and are valid. Both values are within normal range.
