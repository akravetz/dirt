## Photo Observations

### Overview

The SCROG canopy is full, crowded, and actively stretching. Many tops are at or above the net, with the open center gap still visible around the humidifier. Overall posture remains good: no whole-canopy wilt, collapse, or heat-clawing is visible. The main visual risks are density, shaded interior leaves, and root-zone saturation on the plants with high moisture readings.

### Plant A

Plant A continues to carry vigorous sativa-leaning growth, with several upright shoots pushing above the screen. Leaf posture is firm and the active tops look healthy. Color is light-to-medium green under the LED/camera cast, with only minor older-leaf tip or edge wear. Root-zone moisture remains the most normal of the four at about 60%, so the immediate work is stretch tucking rather than dryback intervention.

### Plant B

Plant B remains dense and compact, with broad leaves and crowded interior growth. The upper canopy does not show obvious wilt despite the sensor staying near full saturation. A few older or shaded leaves show pale color and minor tip/edge wear, but the active shoots remain upright. The risk is still below the canopy: B's moisture is rising again and needs manual tray/float/sensor verification.

### Plant C

Plant C has active compact tops near the net, but the lower and interior canopy still shows the clearest yellowing and mottled older-leaf wear. The plant is not collapsed, yet its moisture remains around 90% with little dryback. Treat the lower-leaf decline as a root-zone oxygen, standing-water, airflow, and pH/EC verification problem before increasing feed strength.

### Plant D

Plant D remains a primary purple-leaning keeper candidate with multiple sativa-leaning shoots reaching through the screen. Upper growth is active and the canopy is filling well, while some lower/shaded leaves show lighter color and wear. Moisture remains high but not rising meaningfully today, so keep tucking the taller shoots and watch the tray/lower canopy for wet-air symptoms.

## Flags

### Early-flower VPD stayed below target all day

The environment is wetter than yesterday's lights-on recovery. Overnight averaged 62.44 deg F, 73.36% RH, and 0.51 kPa VPD, which is too cold and too wet for early flower. Morning improved only to 0.90 kPa, and the current 0.95 kPa reading is still below the 1.0-1.3 kPa target. This keeps mold pressure and slow transpiration risk elevated while the canopy is getting denser.

### Plant B remains the highest plant-level risk

B is now 96.83%, up from 96.46% May 5, 96.13% May 4, and 95.51% May 3. The photo still does not show a classic wilt crash, but the sensor trend is no longer compatible with normal dryback. Keep B's feed interrupted and verify tray water, float behavior, root-zone smell, and sensor plausibility before reopening the valve.

### C and D remain saturated root-zone watch items

C is still around 90.5% and D around 86.9%. C has the clearest lower/interior yellowing, while D is structurally more vigorous but still wet below the canopy. Avoid hard defoliation during stretch, but remove dead or trapped leaves that are sitting in humid interior air.

### Canopy density is becoming the airflow constraint

The SCROG is doing its job, but the canopy is now dense enough that lower leaves are shaded and interior air exchange matters. A/D still need daily tucking through stretch. B/C should mostly be allowed to reach up, with only leaf removal for dead, trapped, or wet-contact material.

## Recommendations

1. **Fix the 00-06 environment before adding humidity.** Overnight VPD at 0.51 kPa is the urgent early-flower miss. Increase lights-off clearing/air exchange and avoid any humidifier behavior that keeps RH in the 70s.
2. **Install or verify the low passive intake with a light trap.** The planned 6-inch intake should be checked against the next overnight and lights-on windows. The target is enough fresh/cooler exchange to clear wet air without creating light leaks during 12/12.
3. **Escalate Plant B dryback verification.** Keep B isolated from feed until manual inspection confirms dryback or proves the sensor is wrong. Look for standing tray water, a stuck float, soaked media, sour smell, or roots sitting continuously submerged.
4. **Keep daily SCROG stretch management focused on A and D.** Tuck tall A/D shoots horizontally while stems are still flexible. Do not over-tuck compact B/C tops that are trying to reach the net.
5. **Open lower-canopy airflow selectively.** Remove dead or trapped leaves in wet shade, especially on C and around the densest B/C interiors. Avoid a heavy strip until the stretch window and root-zone behavior are better understood.
6. **Verify reservoir pH/EC at the next service.** The early-flower target remains pH 5.8 and EC 1.4-1.6, but today's report did not capture either value.
7. **Continue conservative clone and Track A seedling management.** Keep clone dome handling conservative until more roots are visible, and keep Track A labels secure while the 7 potted regulars establish.
