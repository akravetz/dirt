## Flags

### Flower flip changes the environment target

Today is Day 0 of 12/12. Temperature is now acceptable for early flower, but RH and VPD are not: 70-74% RH and 0.61-0.91 kPa VPD are too wet for the early-flower target. The first priority after the flip is drying and clearing the tent without creating heat stress.

### Plant B remains critically wet

B is 95.51% now after 94.93% yesterday and 93.90% on May 1. The photo still does not show obvious wilt, but the sustained wet trend is incompatible with the move into flower. Keep/close the B float valve, confirm the tray is not continuously feeding, and reopen only after a clear dryback.

### C and D remain saturated

C is still about 90% and D is still about 87%, with lower/interior yellowing visible in the photos. Upper growth remains active, so this is not yet an acute canopy-wide deficiency signal. Inspect trays and media directly for standing water, sour smell, limp growth after lights-on, or stalled tops.

## Recommendations

1. **Dry the tent for early flower** - target VPD 1.0-1.3 kPa and RH near 45-50% by reducing humidifier contribution, improving exhaust/clearing, and avoiding mist carryover into lights-off.
2. **Keep Plant B on dryback** - keep/close the float valve and verify tray/valve behavior; do not reopen while the sensor remains in the mid-90s unless manual inspection proves the sensor is wrong.
3. **Tuck A and D daily during stretch** - keep the tallest sativa-leaning shoots horizontal under the SCROG during the first 1-2 weeks of 12/12.
4. **Do not defoliate hard yet** - remove only obviously dead or blocking lower leaves if needed for airflow; save heavier lollipopping/defoliation for the established early-flower window.
5. **Continue propagation hygiene** - clones and regular seedlings under the dome still need secure labels and careful venting to avoid damping-off while the main tent RH is high.
