## Photo Observations

The SCROG canopy is dense and actively stretching, with many tops at or above the net and a few open gaps around the center humidifier. Overall posture is good: no whole-plant wilt, collapse, or obvious heat clawing is visible. Color is mostly light-to-medium green under the LED/camera cast, with the main visible problems still concentrated in older shaded interior leaves and saturated root-zone candidates.

### Plant A

Plant A remains one of the most vigorous sativa-leaning contributors to the screen. Several upright shoots are pushing through the net and should keep being tucked horizontally during the early stretch window. Leaf posture looks sound and root-zone moisture remains stable near 60%, with only minor tip/edge wear on older leaves.

Plant B still reads as dense, compact, and broad-leafed with no obvious wilt despite the sensor being in the mid-90s. ### Plant C

Plant C has active upper growth at net height, but the older and lower leaves show the clearest yellowing/mottling in today's set. The plant is still not collapsed, yet the combination of saturated moisture near 90% plus lower-leaf decline keeps root-zone oxygen, standing tray water, and airflow ahead of any feed-strength diagnosis.

### Plant D

Plant D continues to show the sativa-leaning primary-keeper structure, with multiple tops stretching through the SCROG and visible purple petioles/leaf undersides. Upper growth is active, while a few lower or shaded leaves show lighter color and wear. Moisture remains high around 87%, so keep tucking the tall shoots while watching the tray and lower canopy.

## Flags

### Overnight environment regressed wetter

Compared with May 4, the overnight window fell from 65.05 deg F / 63.96% RH / 0.76 kPa VPD to 64.03 deg F / 72.75% RH / 0.56 kPa VPD. That is too wet for early flower and is the clearest environment flag today. The lights-on window is much better: morning VPD is at the 1.0 kPa floor and the current 1.13 kPa reading is in range.

### Plant B remains the highest plant-level risk

B is now 96.46% after 96.13% May 4, 95.51% May 3, and 94.93% May 2. The canopy still does not show obvious wilt, but the root-zone sensor trend says dryback is not established. Manually verify tray water, float behavior, and sensor plausibility before reopening feed.

### C and D remain saturated

C is still around 90% and D around 87%, with lower/interior yellowing visible in the photos. Treat these as root-zone/airflow watch items first. Do not chase the lower leaves with a stronger feed until standing water, tray behavior, and pH/EC are verified.

### Manual breeding, cloning, and intake updates

The SBxBS01 regular batch for Track A has 7 total sprouts from the 10 started seeds. All 7 sprouted seedlings were potted into coco/perlite today for active grow-out; 5 currently look healthy and vigorous, while the remaining 2 are lagging and should be watched before counting them as real pollen candidates. Keep labels secure and follow the isolation-tent culture defaults: hand-watered coco/perlite, runoff removed, and no standing saucer water.

The A/B/C/D mother-plant clones are perking under the humidity dome. Only 1 clone is showing visible roots right now, so the dome stays on and the clone-preservation gate remains open. Keep humidity high, avoid over-handling, and wait for more visible root tips before transplanting to stasis cups.

The high lights-on VPD / humidifier-off behavior was also reviewed against the controller policy. The likely constraint is not a dead humidifier: early-flower RH ceiling behavior can block mist while warmer tent air still drives VPD high. Cooler room air is available outside the mostly zipped tent, and a 6-inch duct was purchased today for a low passive intake install planned on 2026-05-06. Because the grow is on 12/12, install it with a bend/light trap.

## Recommendations

1. **Fix the 00-06 humidity/VPD profile first.** Overnight VPD at 0.56 kPa is the urgent flower-transition miss. Review exhaust/air exchange, humidifier off-gate behavior, and lights-off clearing before adding any humidity.
2. **Install the low passive intake with a light trap.** Use the planned 6-inch duct on 2026-05-06 to bring in cooler room air and reduce intake restriction. After install, compare canopy temp, RH, VPD, and fan behavior in the next lights-on and overnight windows.
3. **Escalate Plant B dryback verification.** Keep B's feed interrupted until manual inspection confirms dryback or proves sensor error. Check for standing tray water and a float that is not actually closed.
4. **Inspect C/D trays and lower canopy airflow.** Remove only dead or trapped leaves that are sitting in wet, shaded air; avoid a hard defoliation until stretch and root-zone behavior are better understood.
5. **Verify reservoir pH/EC at the next change.** The early-flower target is pH 5.8 and EC 1.4-1.6, but today's report did not capture either value.
6. **Keep clone dome management conservative.** With only 1 visible rooted clone so far, keep A/B/C/D cuttings under the humidity dome and transplant only after each rooted cutting has enough visible root mass for stasis.
7. **Track the regular-seedling denominator.** Track A now has 7 sprouted regulars and 5 vigorous seedlings, so expected male count should be based on the surviving/vigorous pool rather than the original 10-seed start.
