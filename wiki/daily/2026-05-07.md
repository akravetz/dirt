The canopy remains full across the SCROG, with many tops now above or pressing through the net. Overall posture is good: no whole-canopy wilt, heat clawing, or collapse is visible. The open center gap is still present, but the plant mass around it is dense enough that lower-canopy airflow and shaded-leaf cleanup matter during the next stretch week.

### Plant A

Plant A is still one of the strongest stretch candidates, with upright sativa-leaning tops above the screen and firm leaf posture. Color reads light-to-medium green under the LED/camera cast, with healthy active tips and only minor older-leaf tip wear visible. Root-zone moisture remains stable near 60%, so A's priority is daily horizontal tucking rather than dryback correction.

Plant B remains compact and crowded, with broad fan leaves and a dense interior. The top growth is upright and does not show obvious wilt even though the moisture sensor remains near saturation. Some shaded older leaves show pale color and minor edge/tip wear, so the risk remains root-zone oxygen and trapped humid air below the canopy rather than a visible canopy crash.

### Plant C

Plant C has active top growth near the screen, but lower and older leaves still show the clearest yellowing/mottling. The plant is not collapsed, and the upper shoots are still moving, but the combination of saturated moisture and shaded interior leaves keeps C on root-zone and airflow watch. Do not treat this as a simple feed-strength issue without checking tray/media conditions and pH/EC.

### Plant D

Plant D remains a primary purple-leaning keeper candidate with multiple sativa-leaning tops pushing through the SCROG. The canopy is filling and upper growth looks active. Lower/shaded leaves show some wear, and moisture is still high, but the reading is easing slightly; keep tucking the tall shoots while watching for wet lower-canopy pockets.

## Flags

### Lights-on VPD recovered, but overnight is still too wet

Compared with May 6, RH improved in every window and lights-on VPD reached the early-flower band: 1.01 kPa morning and 1.07 kPa now. The miss is still the dark window: 63.35 deg F, 69.16% RH, and 0.61 kPa VPD. That is better than yesterday's 0.51 kPa, but still too cool/wet for a dense early-flower canopy.

### Plant B remains the highest root-zone risk

B is finally slightly lower at 96.62% now, but that is not enough dryback to clear the issue after several days above 95%. The photo still does not show a wilt crash, which makes manual verification more important: tray water, float behavior, media smell, root exposure, and sensor plausibility all need to be checked before reopening normal feed.

### C and D are easing but still saturated

C is about 90.0% and D is about 86.3%, both down slightly from May 6. C still shows the clearest lower/interior yellowing, while D remains structurally vigorous. Keep the response conservative: improve airflow, remove dead/trapped leaves, and verify root-zone conditions before changing feed strength.

### Canopy density remains an airflow constraint

The SCROG is full enough that interior humidity pockets can form even while the tent-level VPD improves. A and D should keep being tucked while stems are flexible. B and C should mostly be allowed upward, with only selective cleanup of dead, wet-contact, or deeply trapped leaves.

## Recommendations

1. **Keep the dry/clearing push focused on lights-off.** Today proves lights-on can reach 1.0-1.1 kPa without adding humidity. The remaining control target is raising the 00-06 VPD out of the 0.6 kPa range while avoiding a daytime overshoot.
2. **Verify the 6-inch low passive intake and light trap.** If it was installed, compare tonight's overnight RH/VPD against today. If not, install it low with bends for 12/12 light control and confirm the tent can exchange enough air while zipped.
3. **Keep Plant B isolated from normal feed until dryback is real.** A small drop from 96.83% to 96.62% is directionally good but not a recovery. Manually check the tray/float/media before reopening.
4. **Continue daily SCROG work on A and D.** Tuck tall tops horizontally to fill open squares and keep the canopy even through stretch.
5. **Open the lower canopy selectively.** Remove dead or trapped leaves in humid shade, especially around C and dense B/C interiors. Avoid a heavy strip during active stretch.
6. **Verify reservoir pH/EC at the next service.** Early flower target remains pH 5.8 and EC 1.4-1.6; no pH/EC reading was captured today.
