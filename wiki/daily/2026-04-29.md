## Flags

### 🔴 Plant B moisture escalation

B is now the wettest pot: 91.93% now, up from 86.74% yesterday and 81.84% on Apr 27. That is a fast three-day climb through the elevated zone. No stress is visible in today's photo, but the sensor trend has crossed the watch-and-wait threshold. Close Plant B's float valve today and let the pot dry back before reopening; target a drydown toward the 70–75% range unless manual inspection suggests the sensor is misreading.

### ⚠️ C and D remain saturated but visually stable

C is holding near 89% and D near 87%. Both have tolerated high Autopot moisture for several days without visible wilt or yellowing, so this is monitoring rather than an immediate intervention. If either shows droop, sour media smell, stalled growth, or yellowing, restrict that tray/valve and inspect root-zone aeration.

### ⚠️ H7142 overnight control needs review

Second full overnight window under H7142 control did not improve: overnight RH rose from 65.55% yesterday to 67.41% today, and overnight VPD slipped from 0.80 to 0.76 kPa. Daytime VPD remains usable (morning 1.12, now 0.98 kPa), but the active mist and 64.04% now RH show the tent is still running wetter than the veg RH guide. Review night offset, PI behavior, and any humidity ceiling/off-gate behavior before increasing humidifier output.

### ⚠️ Temperature remains below veg day target

Overnight was just below the 68°F floor, and morning/now stayed under the 74°F day floor. This cool-tent pattern reduces moisture-holding capacity and makes RH/VPD control harder. Reducing exhaust fan speed and stepping the light from 40% to 50% are the two practical levers that also support canopy growth.
