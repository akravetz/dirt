## Key Event: Nutrient Burn on Plant A

**Plant A shows yellowing on upper leaves** identified as **foliar burn** — localized salt damage from accidental nutrient solution droplets landing on leaves, NOT systemic nutrient deficiency or pH problem.

**Diagnosis:** Localized damage pattern (distinct spots rather than uniform yellowing); affects upper leaves only; no progression across other plants → consistent with foliar burn from a nutrient spill.

**Response:** No active intervention needed. Monitor new growth — it should emerge healthy. The burn damage on affected leaves is permanent but will not spread.

- Overwatering / root zone issues

**Action required:** Check and confirm watering solution pH at exactly 5.8 (after nutrients). Ensure not overwatering — 150–200ml only.
